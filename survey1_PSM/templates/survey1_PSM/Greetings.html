You are invited to participate in a research study entitled “ Self-Selection and Good Governance ”, a web-based decision-making experiment. Before taking part in this study, please read the consent form below and click on the “I Agree” button at the bottom of the page if you understand the statements and freely consent to participate in the study.

Consent form

The information on this consent form is provided so that you can decide whether you wish to participate in an experiment at the Behavioral and Experimental Economics Lab at Nazarbayev University. The study involves web-based behavioral experimental games designed to understand decision-making. Participation in the study typically takes 60 minutes and is strictly confidential and no identity-based information will be used or stored. The study will not create any risks for the participants or anyone associates with them. It is important that you understand that your participation is considered voluntary. This means that even if you agree to participate, you are free to withdraw from the experiment at any time.
Participants begin by answering a series of survey questions, after which they play multiple rounds of experimental games. Namely, the Dice task game involves predicting the outcome of a dice roll and public good game which involves sharing a hypothetical amount among multiple players. You will receive a flat remuneration of 2000 KZ Tenge and an additional amount based on your performance in the experiment only after finishing the experiment.
To contact about any further questions, concerns, suggestions, or complaints that are not being addressed by the researcher, you can contact: Zhanar Ashikbayeva, Graduate School of Public Policy, Nazarbayev University. Email: zhanar.ashikbayeva@nu.edu.kz
